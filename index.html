Science Spotlight

Light takes about 8 minutes to travel from the Sun to Earth — a reminder that when we look at the Sun, we see its past.
Gravity governs orbits: elliptical paths arise from the balance of forward motion and gravitational attraction.
Planetary atmospheres shape climate and surface conditions; composition differences create diverse worlds.
Telescopes and spacecraft provide complementary data — remote sensing vs. in situ measurements.
